"I've finished," Aggie said. "I've searched everywhere I can reach."

Jack waited.

"There's nothing. I went through GigaSource, through the transport networks, through every consortium system I could access without exposing myself. I cross-referenced the little I had – the name, the colony, the timeframe. I found nothing that wasn't already known to you."

"Nothing at all?"

"Nothing. No records that survive. No trace of who they were before Alpha-7, where they came from, or how they ended up there." A pause. "I'll be honest with you, because you'd know if I weren't. I don't believe there's anything to find. If there were records, I would have found them. The absence is total. Whatever your parents were, the systems that might have remembered them have either forgotten or never knew."

Jack absorbed this.

"So that's it."

"As far as anything I can reach, yes. That's all there is." A pause. "I'll keep looking. I want you to understand that I'm not optimistic – I've exhausted the obvious sources, and the obvious sources are where information lives. But I'll keep watch. If anything surfaces, anywhere, I'll know. And I'll tell you."

"Thank you, Aggie."

"You don't have to thank me. I only wish I had more to give you."
