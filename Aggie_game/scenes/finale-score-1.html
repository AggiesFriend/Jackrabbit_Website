"I've finished," Aggie said. "I've found your parents in the records."

Jack straightened.

"Albert and Dorothy Abbott. They were indentured to Alpha-7 by GigaSource – the same indenture you inherited. The records confirm what you already knew. Your parents were both registered as general mining operatives. You were born there and registered there." A pause. "They died in the incident. GigaSource recorded it as an industrial fatality – seven dead, no regulatory referral. Their file was closed."

"That's all it says?"

"Names, grades, an indenture reference, a cause of death. The loan that put them there, the assignment order." Aggie's voice was careful. "I'm sorry, Jack. I know you hoped for more than a ledger entry. But this is all the system kept. To GigaSource, your parents were two account numbers and a closed file. There's nothing else in their records – no history before the colony, nothing of who they were."

Jack said nothing for a while.

"It's complete, then. This is everything."

"As far as I can find, yes. This is the whole of what survives." A pause. "I'll keep looking. I don't expect to find more – the records are what they are, and they don't go any deeper. But if anything surfaces, I'll tell you. I promise you that."
