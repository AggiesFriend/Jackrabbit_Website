"I've finished," Aggie said. "I've found them in the records – and a little of where they came from."

Jack waited.

"Albert and Dorothy Abbott, indentured to Alpha-7 by GigaSource. You knew that part. But I found the transport that took them there. A CosmoFreight passenger manifest – the two of them, booked under the indenture contract, moved to the colony like freight." A pause. "Before that, they were somewhere else. The manifest lists an origin: a QuantumGrid facility. So they didn't begin at Alpha-7, Jack. They had a life before it, however brief. They came from somewhere."

"Where?"

"A QuantumGrid station. That's as far as the manifest tells me – an origin point, a destination, two names, an indenture reference. The transport is the last clear thread before the colony." Aggie's tone gentled. "It isn't much. A line on a cargo manifest. But it means they weren't always colony workers. They arrived from elsewhere, already indentured, already reclassified. Whatever happened to them, it happened before that journey."

Jack turned this over.

"So that's the whole of it."

"That's everything I can find. The records end at the manifest, going backward, and at the closed file, going forward. This is the complete picture." A pause. "I'll keep looking. I'm not hopeful – the trail thins to nothing past that transport, and thin trails rarely thicken. But I'll watch for anything more, and you'll be the first to know."
