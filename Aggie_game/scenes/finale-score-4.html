"I've finished," Aggie said. "I've assembled as much as the records allow. I think it is important that you understand what they don't contain."

Jack waited.

"Albert and Dorothy Abbott. Transported to Alpha-7 from a QuantumGrid facility. Before the colony, they worked at that facility – I found their employment records. Your father, a maintenance technician. Your mother, facilities support. They lived in company housing, shared quarters. Ordinary employees with clean records – no trouble, no commendations, nothing remarkable." A pause. "Then GigaSource called a default on a missed loan repayment, reclassified them as labour, and shipped them to the colony. That part you can imagine."

"But?"

"But there's a gap, Jack. Their employment at QuantumGrid is the earliest thing I can find. Before they started there – nothing. No prior employer. No previous address; the records list them as transient. No history of any kind. They appear at QuantumGrid as fully formed adults with no past behind them." Aggie's voice was even, but precise. "When CyberNexus checked their identities for the loan, it noticed the same thing. Shallow records. No secondary sources. I'm telling you what the data tells me and nothing more: wherever your parents were before they arrived at QuantumGrid is not in the records."

Jack was quiet.

"They came from nowhere?"

"From nowhere I can discover. Which is not the same as from nowhere at all – everyone comes from somewhere. But that somewhere is not in any system I've searched." A pause. "This is the complete picture. Everything the records hold, I've found and brought to you. The threads all run back to QuantumGrid and stop." Another pause. "I'll keep looking. I'll be honest – I don't think I'll find what's missing. The gap is too clean to be an accident of record-keeping, and clean gaps don't fill themselves. But I'll keep searching. If their past exists anywhere, I'll find it."
