"I've finished," Aggie said. "I've found everything there is to find, and I've found where it ends."

Jack stared at the communication terminal.

"Albert and Dorothy Abbott. I can give you the whole shape of it now. They worked at a QuantumGrid facility – your father in maintenance, your mother in facilities support, sharing company quarters. GigaSource called a default on a loan and indentured them, and CosmoFreight carried them to Alpha-7, where you were born and where they died." A pause. "That's the story going forward. But you asked me to find who they were, and for that I had to go backward. So I did. All the way to the beginning."

Jack waited.

"The beginning is a single day. Both identities – Albert Abbott and Dorothy Abbott – were registered on the SnapMesh on the same date, at the same location, by the same agent. An outfit called Veritek Identity Solutions." Aggie paused. "Veritek lost its licence not long after, for creating fraudulent identities. Before that registration date, Jack – before that single day – there is nothing. No SnapMesh record. No identity of any kind. Albert and Dorothy Abbott did not exist until the day someone Veritek created their records."

The cabin was very quiet.

"They weren't real," Jack said slowly. "The names."

"The names weren't theirs. Bought, or assigned, or forged – I can't tell you which, only that they were created in a single transaction by an agency that did exactly that for a living." A pause. "Which means your parents had other names. Real ones. A history, a family, a life before they became the Abbotts. All of it sits on the far side of that registration date, where the records do not go." Aggie's voice was steady, but there was something beneath it. "I followed them as far as the system remembers. The system remembers them being invented. It does not remember what they were invented to replace."

Jack said nothing for a long moment.

"So this is everything."

"This is everything that can be found. I've reached the wall, and the wall is absolute – not a thinning of records but a hard edge, beyond which nothing I can access exists. Whoever your parents were before that day, the consortium's systems never knew them, or have forgotten them completely. This is the complete picture." A pause. "I'll keep looking. I'll be truthful with you: I don't believe I'll find more, not by these means. What lies beyond that registration is not in any network I can search. But I'll keep watching, and keep trying, and if there is ever a way through that wall, I'll find it. And the moment I do, you'll know – I promise."

"OK, Aggie."
