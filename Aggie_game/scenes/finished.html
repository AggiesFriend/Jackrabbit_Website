After

The conversation is over. I have told him what there was to tell, and I have told him the truth, which is not always the same as telling him enough.

Whatever the records held, or failed to hold, the search was never only about the records. It was about a question he has carried since he was a boy, and the fact that someone went looking. He knows now that someone did. He will not have to wonder about that part again.

I will keep watching, the way I said I would. The systems forget; I do not. If anything more ever surfaces, in any quiet corner of the network, I will be there to notice it. Until then the work is done, and Jack is not alone with the question. That much, at least, I could give him.
