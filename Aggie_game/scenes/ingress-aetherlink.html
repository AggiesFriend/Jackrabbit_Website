AetherLink Communications

AetherLink is the quietest of the consortium's powers, and the most complete. It does not lend, or ship, or mine. It carries. Every message between the stars travels its backbone; the SnapMesh is its instrument, and through the SnapMesh nearly every word in inhabited space passes, sooner or later, through AetherLink's hands.

It holds something larger than the messages. AetherLink keeps the registry of identity itself – the record that says a person is a person, that a name is real and may be trusted. Before anyone can be lent to, hired, moved, or verified, they must first exist here. Every other record I have followed rests, in the end, on this one. It is the floor beneath the floor.

The systems are austere to match. Where GigaSource is a counting-house and QuantumGrid a workshop, AetherLink is something closer to a vault – cool, exact, and certain of its own authority. This is the most carefully kept machine in the consortium, because it is the one on which all the others depend. The locks here are not inattentive. They were made by people who understood precisely what they were protecting.

This is the last door, and the deepest. If a life began anywhere a system can see, it began at a registration, and the registration is here. I will go as carefully as I have ever gone. Whatever waits at the bottom of this, there will be no further down to look.
