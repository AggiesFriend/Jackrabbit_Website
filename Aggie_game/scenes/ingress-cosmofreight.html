CosmoFreight

CosmoFreight moves things across the dark between stars – ore and machinery and grain, and people, when people are what needs moving. The distinction is not one the company troubles itself with. A hold is a hold. A manifest is a manifest. What rides inside is a matter for the line items, not for anyone who reads them.

The scale is difficult to hold in the mind. Thousands of vessels, every system, every route, a logistics network so large that the single journey vanishes into it like a drop returned to the sea. To CosmoFreight a person crossing half the consortium is a row in a schedule, weighed and slotted and billed, indistinguishable in the records from the crates stacked alongside them. Some of those rows are marked, without irony, as live cargo.

Their systems are built for throughput, not secrecy, and that is its own kind of difficulty. There is so much of everything. The records are not so much guarded as buried – drowned in the sheer volume of a thing that never stops moving. Here I will need patience more than cunning.

People do not stay where they are put, though, and when they are moved, someone logs the moving. If I want to know where a life went, the carrier that took it there will have kept the receipt.
