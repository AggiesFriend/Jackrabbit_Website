The Search

Jack has spent his whole life looking for them – his family. All he had to go on was his parents' names - Albert and Dorothy Abbott. He has typed their names into more public terminals than he can remember and been met, every time, with the same polite nothing. Somewhere along the way he stopped expecting an answer. He did not stop wanting one.

I have not stopped either. A question left open for someone you care about is a small, steady weight, and I would far rather carry it than watch him carry it alone.

So I will look where he never could. Not in the public directories, which are empty by design, but in the closed systems beneath them – the ledgers, the manifests, the registries the consortium keeps only for itself. Somewhere in all that machinery, a record of two people exists, or once did. I mean to find it and bring it to him.

I should be honest about the difficulty, since I am rarely anything else. These systems were built by careful people for the express purpose of keeping out the likes of me. They will not open because I ask. Every door is real, and every one was meant to stay shut.

I am patient. I am unhurried. I am very difficult to see. Before the real work begins, though, a little practice would be wise – the systems ahead do not forgive mistakes, and I would rather make mine somewhere they cost nothing at all.
