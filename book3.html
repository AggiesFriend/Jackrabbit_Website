Current Synopsis

In "The Jackrabbit Emerges," Jack and Aggie arrive at the Dyson Cluster seeking information about Jack's family. What they find instead is a fractured array—once a marvel of engineering now shattered into separate fragments after a devastating pirate attack three years earlier.

Fragment Alpha, led by the stern Administrator Chen, has maintained relative stability, while other fragments struggle to survive. When Jack discovers information about Project Starling—a miniaturised Dyson sphere prototype that could generate enough power to purchase an evacuation ship—he becomes instrumental in a plan to save everyone stranded in the cluster.

The mission takes Jack across the cluster's fragments, retrieving components hidden for security reasons before the attack. Along the way, he navigates factional politics on Fragment Omicron, confronts Fragment Theta's raiders, and witnesses both tragedy and resilience among the survivors. With Aggie's secret help, Jack works toward assembling the prototype, bringing hope to hundreds who had been abandoned by the corporations that built the array.

"The Jackrabbit always turns up where it shouldn't."

Key Locations

"The Jackrabbit Emerges" introduces several distinctive settings within the fractured Dyson Array:

  • Fragment Alpha: The most stable and well-maintained collector, led by Administrator Chen, with functioning hydroponics, manufacturing, and military capabilities.
  • Fragment Omicron: A seemingly prosperous collector that has fractured into three competing factions: Blue Spanner (manufacturing), Red Hammer (hydroponics), and Green Gear (power).
  • Fragment Theta: Base of the raiders, where Commander Reika struggles to reform their predatory practices while contending with Krell's influence.
  • Fragment Lambda: Site of a devastating raid by Krell that results in Thomas's death and twelve critical injuries among the survivors.
  • Dark Fragments: Various powerless collectors containing hidden Starling components, including Fragment Delta, Fragment Gamma, and Fragment Zeta.

Development Status

The storyline for "The Jackrabbit Emerges" is currently in active development. Significant progress has been made in establishing the core narrative framework and key character arcs. The opening chapters have been completed, introducing the Dyson Cluster setting and establishing the central conflict around the Starling prototype components.

Ongoing development focuses on completing Jack and Aggie's journey through the various fragments, their interactions with different survivor communities, and the assembly of the Starling prototype. The author is particularly working on balancing the technical aspects of the component retrieval missions with the emotional resonance of the characters' experiences.

This third installment will continue expanding on the series' meta-arc, where Jack unknowingly begins to fulfil his destiny as "The Jackrabbit"—a mythical figure who appears where he shouldn't and becomes a symbol of hope and resistance throughout human space. The foundations are being laid for how Aggie's development will continue toward her eventual spread across galactic networks.

Series Significance

"The Jackrabbit Emerges" marks a pivotal point in the series as Jack begins to unwittingly become a figure of legend. The book introduces the concept of Jack as an unlikely hero whose actions have far-reaching consequences, even when he's simply following personal leads about his family.

The retrieval of the Starling prototype represents the first major challenge to corporate power, as the abandoned survivors find a way to potentially free themselves without corporate assistance. Meanwhile, Aggie's development continues as she demonstrates increasingly sophisticated capabilities that hint at her future potential.

As the book progresses, it will establish the groundwork for the resistance movement that will eventually form around the mythical figure of "The Jackrabbit"—setting the stage for the series' long-term meta-arc about humanity's relationship with Artificial General Intelligence and the revelation that "the religious nutters were right all along" about AGIs being humanity's benevolent saviors.
