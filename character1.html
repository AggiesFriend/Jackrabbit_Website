Jack Abbott

Protagonist of the Jackrabbit series

Jack Abbott

Protagonist

Biography

Born and raised in the GigaSource mining colony on Asteroid Alpha-7, Jack has never known life outside the oppressive confines of the mine. Despite his youth, he is a survivor who has developed remarkable skills navigating the colony's tunnels while avoiding guards. After discovering a mysterious device that leads him to a forgotten airlock, Jack's life changes forever when he meets Aggie and glimpses the vastness of space for the first time. Following the tragic death of his parents in a mining explosion, Jack resolves to escape the only home he's ever known and forge a new life among the stars.

In "Wings of Freedom," Jack adjusts to life on Horizon Outpost while Aggie's ship undergoes extensive repairs. He enrolls in a pilot training course where he displays an extraordinary natural talent for flying, excelling far beyond his classmates' abilities and earning both admiration and resentment. His friendship with Brinn deepens, yet also reveals the growing gulf between their experiences and perspectives on life. As Jack takes on freelance courier jobs with the newly christened Jackrabbit, he encounters the harsh realities of life beyond corporate control at places like Dead End Drift, causing him to mature rapidly. His encounter with a mercenary fleet hunting his ship leads to a daring escape and the realization that the wider universe is both more wondrous and more dangerous than he had imagined.

Personality Traits

Resourceful Inquisitive Adaptable Resilient Cautious Determined

Appearances

  • Stolen Freedom - As the main protagonist, Jack's journey from a "tunnel rat" in the mining colony to an escapee discovering the wider universe forms the backbone of the story.
  • Wings of Freedom - Jack continues as the protagonist, developing his piloting skills and establishing himself as a courier while experiencing both the wonders and harsh realities of life beyond corporate control. His natural talent for flying and growing independence mark his continuing evolution from survivor to skilled pilot.

Key Relationships

Aggie (AGI)

Jack forms an unlikely alliance with Aggie, an artificial general intelligence housed in a damaged courier ship. Their partnership evolves from mutual necessity to genuine friendship as they work together to escape the mining colony. In "Wings of Freedom," their bond deepens even during Aggie's hibernation. When she reveals her true nature as an AGI to Jack, his immediate acceptance and loyalty demonstrate the depth of their relationship.

Albert & Dorothy Abbott

Jack's parents, indentured miners who sacrificed everything to provide what little comfort they could for their son. Their tragic deaths in a mining explosion becomes a catalyst for Jack's escape. In "Wings of Freedom," Jack begins actively seeking information about his family history, suggesting they remain important to his identity despite their absence.

Brinn

A jovial boy Jack meets at Horizon Outpost who introduces him to normal childhood experiences like laughter and friendship. In "Wings of Freedom," their friendship deepens but also reveals the growing gulf between their perspectives, culminating in a poignant farewell when Jack recognises that their paths must diverge.

Captain Marcus Hale

Jack's pilot instructor who recognises and nurtures his exceptional flying abilities while maintaining a professional distance. Hale becomes an important mentor figure, pushing Jack to develop his talents by secretly providing him with advanced training scenarios.

Foreman Oswald (Ozzy)

The shipyard foreman who repairs and upgrades Jack's ship, dubbing it the "Jackrabbit." Ozzy develops a paternal interest in Jack, connecting him with useful contacts and providing guidance that extends beyond ship maintenance.

Chas Drayton

A fellow pilot trainee and antagonist whose family name links back to Victor Drayton. Chas's resentment of Jack's natural piloting abilities creates tension throughout their training, serving as both obstacle and motivation for Jack to excel.
