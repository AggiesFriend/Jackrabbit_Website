Victor Drayton

Antagonist / Mine Foreman

Biography

The younger son of a senior GigaSource executive, Victor Drayton lived a life of privilege and excess until a catastrophic incident where, while under the influence of hallucinogens, he crashed a land-car into a crowd, killing and maiming several people. Rather than face justice, his father arranged for the charges to be dismissed and exiled him to oversee one of GigaSource's most remote mining colonies. Far from corporate oversight, Drayton has established himself as a brutal dictator over the colony, ruling through fear and executing workers on a whim. His sadistic tendencies, coupled with his paranoia about perceived corporate inspections, make him a dangerous and unpredictable force in the lives of the miners.

Personality Traits

Cruel Narcissistic Paranoid Hedonistic Entitled Sadistic

Appearances

  • Stolen Freedom - Victor serves as the primary antagonist, embodying the oppressive regime of GigaSource and representing the brutality Jack has lived under his entire life. His paranoia about inspections and his luxurious shuttle ultimately provide key elements needed for Jack and Aggie's escape.

Key Relationships

The Mine Workers

Drayton views the miners as expendable resources, treating them with contempt and cruelty. He enjoys demonstrating his absolute power over them through public executions and arbitrary punishments.

Security Personnel

The colony's guards are extensions of Drayton's will, carrying out his orders without question and enabling his reign of terror. Their loyalty is secured through fear rather than respect.

Jack Abbott

While never directly interacting until their final confrontation, Drayton's regime has shaped Jack's entire life. Their brief but violent encounter aboard Drayton's shuttle represents Jack's symbolic rejection of the oppression he has lived under.

The Executive Retreat

Drayton's personal shuttle, the "Executive Retreat," is a symbol of his excess and privilege amid the squalor of the mining colony. The vessel represents his only connection to his previous life of luxury and becomes a key element in Jack and Aggie's escape plan.
