Katherine Keller

Aletheion Logistics Executive / Antagonist

Biography

Katherine Keller is the Executive Vice President of Aletheion Logistics, representing the cold, calculating face of corporate authority. Having climbed the corporate ladder through decades of ruthless efficiency and political maneuvering, she embodies the institutional power that shapes the lives of employees without regard for their individual humanity.

Keller's approach to management is purely transactional—employees are resources to be optimised, not people to be nurtured. She views exceptional performance as something to be leveraged for corporate advantage rather than celebrated for personal achievement. Her oversight of courier operations is marked by strict adherence to protocols and an unwavering focus on maximising profitability.

When Jack Abbott's extraordinary abilities come to her attention, Keller sees an asset to be exploited rather than a person to be developed. Her decision to promote Jack to Tier 4 courier status is driven entirely by corporate interests, with no consideration for how such rapid advancement might affect him personally. She represents the institutional indifference that treats human potential as just another commodity.

Keller's interactions with subordinates are characterised by professional detachment and barely concealed impatience with anything that doesn't directly serve corporate objectives. She serves as a stark contrast to the warmth and genuine care that Jack experiences with the Kowalski family, highlighting the difference between institutional power and human compassion.

Key Relationships

Jack Abbott

Keller views Jack purely as a corporate asset to be exploited for Aletheion's benefit. Her promotion of him to Tier 4 is based entirely on his potential value to the corporation, with no consideration for his personal wellbeing or development as an individual.

Director Halsey

Halsey reports to Keller on Jack's advancement through the courier ranks. While Halsey may have more personal interest in Jack's development, Keller's focus remains purely on the corporate benefits of his exceptional abilities.

Frank Kowalski

Though they likely have little direct interaction, Keller represents everything that Frank has learned to navigate during his decades at Aletheion—the corporate machinery that values efficiency over humanity and sees employees as expendable resources.
